# MassAbility Services for Students and Youth

MassAbility offers diverse support for students and youth with disabilities transitioning from high school. Career services focus on employment goals, while home and community programs develop leadership and independent living skills.

[Apply for services today](https://massmrc.hylandcloud.com/customcode/mrconemrc)

## Career Services

### [Pre-Employment Transition Services (Pre-ETS)](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/pre-employment-transition-services-pre-ets-0)Pre-Employment Transition Services (Pre-ETS)   +

**\*Ages 14 - 21 years old****(up to their 22nd birthday)**

Services include job exploration counseling, workplace readiness training, work-based learning experience, counseling in post-secondary training opportunities, and self-advocacy.

[Learn Moreabout Pre-Employment Transition Services (Pre-ETS)](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/pre-employment-transition-services-pre-ets-0 "learn more about Pre-Employment Transition Services (Pre-ETS)   ")

### [Career Services](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/massability-career-services)Career Services+

**Two years prior to high school graduation**

Career Services can help students and youth prepare for a career through: assessment, counseling and guidance, training, education, assistive technology, and etc.

[Learn Moreabout Career Services](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/massability-career-services "learn more about Career Services")

### [Benefits Counseling](https://www.mass.gov/benefits-counseling)Benefits Counseling+

**Ages 16 - adulthood**

Consultation and information for those receiving SSI/SSDI benefits, who need help understanding eligibility and the impact work may have on their benefits.

[Learn Moreabout Benefits Counseling](https://www.mass.gov/benefits-counseling "learn more about Benefits Counseling")

### [Youth Leadership Network (YLN](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/youth-leadership-network-yln)Youth Leadership Network (YLN+

**Ages 14 - 26**

Regional workshops throughout Massachusetts on topics such as leadership, self-determination, peer networking, assistive technology in the workplace, and employment skills.

[Learn Moreabout Youth Leadership Network (YLN](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/youth-leadership-network-yln "learn more about Youth Leadership Network (YLN")

[American Student Assistance (ASA) – resource tool](https://www.asa.org/?show=pitch)

To learn more about any of these services [contact the nearest Career Services office](https://www.mass.gov/orgs/massability/locations).

## Home and Community Life Services

### [Supported Living Program (SLP)](https://www.mass.gov/supported-living-program)Supported Living Program (SLP)+

**Ages 16 - adulthood**

The Supported Living Program provides youth and adults with physical disabilities and an additional secondary disability with assistance in managing and organizing day-to-day life. Services may include case management, PCA surrogacy, assistance with managing health, finances, personal affairs, skills training and finding accessible housing. Services can start one year before high school completion and can be on-going.

[Learn Moreabout Supported Living Program (SLP)](https://www.mass.gov/supported-living-program "learn more about Supported Living Program (SLP)")

### [Transition to Adulthood Program (TAP)](https://www.mass.gov/independent-living-centers)Transition to Adulthood Program (TAP)+

**Ages 14 - 21 years old****(up to their 22nd birthday)**

Through Independent Living Centers, the TAP provides skills training, peer counseling, and mentoring to support young adults to live independently in the community of their choice.

[Learn Moreabout Transition to Adulthood Program (TAP)](https://www.mass.gov/independent-living-centers "learn more about Transition to Adulthood Program (TAP)")

### [Statewide Head Injury Program (SHIP)](https://www.mass.gov/statewide-head-injury-program-ship)Statewide Head Injury Program (SHIP)+

**Ages 18 - adulthood (limited services available to consumers starting at birth)**

Offers an array of services that are designed to assist youth with traumatic brain injuries maintain and increase their level of independence at home.

[Learn Moreabout Statewide Head Injury Program (SHIP)](https://www.mass.gov/statewide-head-injury-program-ship "learn more about Statewide Head Injury Program (SHIP)")

\*Students are defined as 14-21 years old (up to their 22nd birthday) and enrolled in high school or post-secondary education or training programs
